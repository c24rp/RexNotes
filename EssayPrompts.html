Essay Prompts

  1. Peter doesn’t understand Jerry, but does Jerry understand Peter? Does Jerry want to be understood? What can this play teach us about language and communication?
  2. Does The Zoo Story fall under the classification of the Theatre of the Absurd? Does it have a lesson at the end? Is it a comedy?
  3. The characters in The Zoo Story frequently refer to the play’s New York setting. What does Albee accomplish by so heavily emphasizing the setting?
  4. How does Peter’s attitude about the bench changes over the course of the play? What point is Albee trying to make?
  5. Analyze the play through a queer lens. What choices does Albee make when depicting homosexuality? How is your analysis impacted knowing that he himself is gay?
